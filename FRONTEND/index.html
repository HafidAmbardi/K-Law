Wage Disparity in the Shadows of Success

Addressed to

CUBE

As a backup dancer in the K-pop industry, I've witnessed firsthand the stark wage disparity that plagues our profession. Despite contributing to the success of countless performances, we are often paid mere fractions of what the idols earn. Our hard work and dedication are undervalued, leaving us struggling to make ends meet while the spotlight shines brightly on others. This wage inequality perpetuates a system where the labor of many is exploited for the profit of a few, highlighting the urgent need for fair compensation across all roles within the industry. ...

Despite these challenges, many of us continue to pursue our passion for dance, driven by a love for our craft and a desire to share our talents with the world. However, without fair compensation and recognition for our contributions, it becomes increasingly difficult to sustain a career in an industry that thrives on our talent and dedication. It's time for the K-pop industry to acknowledge the vital role that backup dancers play in its success and ensure that we are compensated fairly for our hard work and dedication.
